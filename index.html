What Are Fuel Cells?

Fuel cells generate electricity through an electrochemical reaction, rather than combustion. They use hydrogen or ethanol to produce clean, efficient power with zero harmful emissions.

Zero Emissions

Unlike traditional engines, fuel cells emit only water vapor, making them environmentally friendly.

High Efficiency

Fuel cells convert chemical energy directly into electricity, reducing energy losses compared to combustion engines.

Reliable Power

Fuel cells provide consistent power output, making them ideal for backup systems and industrial applications.

Fuel Cells vs. Traditional Engines

Feature Fuel Cells Traditional Engines
Efficiency Up to 60% 20-30%
Emissions Zero (Only Water Vapor) CO₂, NOₓ, and Particulates
Maintenance Low (No Moving Parts) High (Complex Mechanics)
Lifespan Longer Due to Fewer Components Shorter Due to Wear and Tear
